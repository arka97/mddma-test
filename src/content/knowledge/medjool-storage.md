# Medjool Dates — storage, handling and shelf-life

Medjool is a soft, high-moisture date. Handled well it commands the top of the retail shelf; handled badly it sugars, ferments or dries within weeks. Here is the operator's checklist that MDDMA members use.

## Baseline conditions

- **Ideal temperature:** 4–7 °C for long storage; up to 18 °C for short-term display.
- **Relative humidity:** 60–65%. Below 50% the fruit dries; above 75% it moulds.
- **Light:** Keep out of direct sun — UV darkens the skin.

## Container to warehouse

- **Reefer temperature at Nhava Sheva:** log and photograph the reefer thermometer at the time of de-stuffing.
- **Transit shock:** offload within 6 hours of gate-out; do not let containers sit on the yard in Mumbai summer.
- **Immediate re-cool:** the mandi cold-storage should be at 4 °C when the pallet enters.

## Retail display (up to 3 weeks)

- Store in the display fridge overnight; put on ambient shelves in the morning.
- Rotate stock daily — pack date visible.
- Never mix crop years on the same tray.

## Signs of trouble

| Sign | What it means | Action |
| ---- | ------------- | ------ |
| White sugar crystals | Sugar migration — normal cosmetic issue | Warm gently, mix back in |
| Fizzy / alcoholic smell | Fermentation — moisture too high | Discard batch |
| Hard, wrinkled skin | Dried out — humidity too low | Rehumidify, re-pack, sell fast |
| Grey/green mould | Contamination — humidity way too high | Discard, sanitise storage |

## Packing for onward sale

Use food-grade PET or corrugated with a moisture barrier. Vacuum-packing extends shelf life to 12 months at 4 °C. All MDDMA-branded storefronts follow FSSAI packing declarations — check the [brands catalogue](/brands) for accredited packers.
