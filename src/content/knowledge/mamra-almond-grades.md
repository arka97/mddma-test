# Mamra Almond Grades — a buyer's field guide

Mamra almonds are the Rolls-Royce of the Indian almond trade. Grown in the mountainous belts of Iran and Afghanistan, they sell at a 3–5× premium to Californian *Nonpareil* on the Vashi market. This is a quick, no-nonsense guide for buyers who are new to the category.

## What "Mamra" actually means

Mamra is not a single cultivar — it is a **shape class**. True Mamra kernels are:

- **Irregularly shaped** — humped, curved, sometimes flat on one side.
- **Golden-brown** with a slightly oily sheen.
- **Denser** than Californians — they sink in water instead of floating.
- **Lower moisture** (around 4–5%) and higher fat.

If a lot looks perfectly uniform and pointed, it is almost certainly not Mamra.

## The three commercial tiers

| Tier | Origin | Typical price band* | Visual cue |
| ---- | ------ | ------------------- | ---------- |
| Mamra Iran (A grade) | Yazd, Fars, Chahar Mahal | Highest | Deep golden, oily sheen |
| Mamra Afghan | Herat, Kunduz | High | Slightly lighter, sometimes chalky |
| Sanora / Californian | USA | Base | Uniform, pale, floats |

<small>*Ranges only — exact prices depend on crop year, INR/USD and origin lot.*</small>

## What to check at the mandi

1. **Weight in hand** — a real Mamra kernel feels heavier than it looks.
2. **Break test** — the kernel should snap crisply, not bend.
3. **Sheen** — natural oiliness, not surface polish.
4. **Origin invoice** — always ask for the import bill (BOE) or the local supplier's origin certificate.
