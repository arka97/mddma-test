# Dry-fruit import basics — HS codes, duty and licences

Importing dates, almonds or pistachios into India is not hard, but it is document-heavy. This is the minimum stack a first-time importer needs. Talk to your CHA and a MDDMA member for lot-specific rates before you commit.

## The four licences you must have

1. **IEC** — Importer-Exporter Code from DGFT. One-time, free, 100% online.
2. **FSSAI Central Licence** — mandatory for all food imports. Category: "Importer".
3. **APEDA registration** — required if you plan to re-export.
4. **AD-Code registration** at your port of import — links your bank to the port.

Without any one of these, your bill of entry (BOE) will not clear.

## Common HS codes

| Product | HS Code | Typical BCD |
| ------- | ------- | ----------- |
| Almonds in shell | 0802 11 00 | ₹35/kg |
| Almonds shelled | 0802 12 00 | ₹100/kg |
| Pistachios in shell | 0802 51 00 | 10% |
| Pistachios shelled | 0802 52 00 | 10% |
| Cashew kernels (W320, W240) | 0801 32 10 | 45% + cess |
| Dates fresh/dried | 0804 10 20 | 20% + cess |
| Walnuts shelled | 0802 32 00 | 100% |
| Raisins | 0806 20 10 | 100% |

<small>Rates change with each budget — always confirm live rates on ICEGATE before quoting.</small>

## The paperwork per shipment

- Commercial invoice + packing list
- Bill of lading (or airway bill)
- Certificate of Origin — GSP where applicable
- Phytosanitary Certificate from origin country
- FSSAI clearance at port (physical or documentary)
- Insurance certificate
- Country-specific quality certificates (e.g. USDA for Californian almonds)

## Quality release

FSSAI at Nhava Sheva usually clears in 3–5 working days for a known importer. First-time importers are almost always sampled. Book cold-storage in advance — a Medjool container held at 40 °C on the yard for a week will ferment.
